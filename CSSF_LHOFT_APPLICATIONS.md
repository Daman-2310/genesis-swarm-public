# Borrowed Trust — CSSF Innovation Hub + LHoFT application drafts

The fastest credibility a 16-year-old solo founder in Luxembourg can acquire is
**borrowed from the regulator's own programmes**. Two targets, both free, both
worth more on your homepage than any feature:

1. **LHoFT** — Luxembourg House of Financial Technology (the national fintech hub/community).
2. **CSSF Innovation Hub** — the regulator's own contact point for innovative projects.

**Honesty rule:** until one of these confirms in writing, do NOT put "CSSF" or
"LHoFT" logos / "member of" claims on the site. Overclaiming a regulatory
affiliation is exactly the kind of thing that ends a RegTech company. What you
*can* say truthfully today is below in "Truthful credibility lines."

---

## 1. LHoFT — community / startup onboarding

**Who they are:** Luxembourg's fintech hub — community membership, startup
programmes, ecosystem intros (to funds, banks, fiduciaires, investors).
**Why you fit:** EU-fund RegTech built in Luxembourg, to CSSF/AIFMD frameworks.
**How to apply:** lhoft.com → "Join" / "Community" / contact form. Also worth a
direct, short email.

**Draft email:**

> Subject: 16-year-old Luxembourg founder — RegTech for CSSF/AIFMD fund compliance
>
> Hi LHoFT team,
>
> I'm Daman Sharma, a 16-year-old founder based in Luxembourg. I've built
> **Genesis Swarm**, a RegTech platform for European fund administrators —
> deterministic, in-browser compliance engines for AIFMD II, CSSF Circulars
> 24/856 and 18/698, plus a tamper-evident, Bitcoin-anchorable audit trail.
>
> It's live and open-source: a compliance officer can paste a fund prospectus and
> get an AIFMD II verdict in ~300ms — including catching a prospectus that permits
> more leverage than the law allows — with every check cited to the underlying
> regulation. No backend, no LLM guesswork.
>
> - Live demo: https://genesis-swarm-rgq5.vercel.app/scan
> - Source: https://github.com/Daman-2310/genesis-swarm-public
>
> I'd love to join the LHoFT community and get feedback from people who know the
> Luxembourg fund world. Could we have a short call?
>
> Daman Sharma · daman.sharma.2310@gmail.com

## 2. CSSF Innovation Hub — regulator dialogue

**Who they are:** the CSSF's channel for firms with innovative financial-sector
projects to engage the regulator early. It is a *dialogue*, not an endorsement —
set expectations accordingly. Reaching out is itself a credibility signal ("we
engaged the regulator about our approach").
**How:** cssf.lu → search "Innovation hub" / "FinTech" → the innovation contact
form / email listed there.

**Draft message:**

> Subject: Innovation Hub enquiry — automated AIFMD II / CSSF 24/856 compliance tooling
>
> Dear CSSF Innovation Hub,
>
> I'm developing Genesis Swarm, a RegTech tool that performs deterministic,
> auditable checks of fund documentation against AIFMD II leverage/retention/
> concentration limits and the substance expectations of Circular 24/856, and
> maintains a tamper-evident (SHA-256 hash-chained, Bitcoin-anchorable) record of
> every check performed.
>
> I would value the Innovation Hub's guidance on two points:
> 1. Whether an *independent, advisory* compliance-checking tool of this kind
>    raises any supervisory considerations I should design for now.
> 2. How best to position language so the tool is clearly decision-support, not a
>    substitute for the AIFM's own responsibility.
>
> The tool is live and open-source for inspection:
> https://genesis-swarm-rgq5.vercel.app/lux — source:
> https://github.com/Daman-2310/genesis-swarm-public
>
> Thank you for your time.
> Daman Sharma · daman.sharma.2310@gmail.com

*(Note the deliberately humble framing — you're asking for guidance, not claiming
compliance. Regulators respond far better to that.)*

## Truthful credibility lines you CAN use today

All verifiable, no permission needed:

- "Open-source — full engine code public on GitHub, no NDA for technical review."
- "Deterministic compliance logic — no LLM in the verdict path, every result reproducible."
- "Built to AIFMD II (Directive (EU) 2024/927), CSSF Circulars 24/856 & 18/698, eIDAS, DORA."
- "Tamper-evident audit trail — SHA-256 hash-chains, anchorable to Bitcoin via OpenTimestamps."
- "Live entity verification against the GLEIF global LEI registry."
- "73 automated tests; engines verified against worked examples."

## Sequence

1. Send the LHoFT email first (lowest friction, community = warm intros).
2. Send the CSSF Innovation Hub enquiry (slower, but a single reply is gold).
3. The moment either confirms, add a *truthful* line to the homepage
   (e.g. "In dialogue with the CSSF Innovation Hub" only once that's literally true).
